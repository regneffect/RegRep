# Grant Skins: Universal Spine + Funder-Specific Paragraphs

## Universal Spine Paragraph (8-12 Sentences)

Court-Connected Recovery Bridge (CCRB) is a court-to-community recovery continuum serving transitional age youth ages 12-24 with opioid/substance use involvement who are identified through Harris County juvenile and young adult courts and affiliated juvenile probation referral pathways. CCRB deploys a structured 90-day stabilization model followed by up to 12 months of APG-informed Youth Recovery Community (YRC) continuing care. The program begins with standardized intake and screening, including educational status and universal literacy screening, and then delivers peer-led recovery coaching and adolescent-focused evidence-based elements (MET/CBT, contingency management, and A-CRA-informed supports) to stabilize substance use and re-engage youth in school/work pathways. For eligible youth ages 16+ with suspected opioid use disorder, CCRB prioritizes rapid medication linkage through a telehealth-enabled MOUD assessment pathway with a 14-day target. CCRB embeds harm reduction by pairing fentanyl/naloxone education with naloxone distribution to youth and family members. CCRB also treats family stabilization as recovery infrastructure through Certified Family Partner navigation, home visits, parent education, and connections to benefits, housing, legal supports, and community behavioral health services. CCRB is delivered through a consortium model where Grace Solutions International serves as fiscal agent and compliance backbone, Credible Messengers United provides program operations and peer recovery delivery, and Strategic Recovery Solutions delivers MOUD navigation and Certified Family Partner services, with UTHealth Houston as evaluation partner. CCRB tracks timely assessment, treatment initiation, stabilization completion, continuing-care engagement, family engagement, and naloxone distribution as measurable outputs and outcomes.

## OAFC Skin

OAFC funding will be used to operate CCRB's opioid abatement service lane for justice-involved transitional age youth by supporting the defined 90-day stabilization workflow and the 12-month YRC continuing-care structure. This lane prioritizes rapid MOUD assessment linkage for eligible youth ages 16+ (14-day target), peer-delivered stabilization supports using MET/CBT and contingency management elements, and family-level stabilization through Certified Family Partner education, navigation, and home-based supports. OAFC dollars will also fund naloxone education and distribution and the participant support items that are budgeted as direct CCRB stabilization resources. CCRB will maintain distinct cost objectives, reimbursement documentation, and monitoring artifacts so OAFC funds are not used for any cost reimbursed by another funding source, consistent with OAFC non-duplication requirements.

## Hogg Skin [NEEDED: exact Hogg RFP/initiative]

Hogg funding will be used only for CCRB's non-clinical capacity deliverables that strengthen mental health and family-support infrastructure without duplicating OAFC-funded direct services. This includes building CCRB's training and supervision system for peer and family-support roles, formalizing trauma-informed practice standards and referral workflows, and implementing a durable data/QA operating system (dashboards, documentation standards, fidelity tools, and learning routines) that improves continuity of care from court to community. All Hogg-funded work will be coded to capacity-only cost objectives and produce auditable artifacts (manuals, training rosters, QA logs, and partnership protocols), with explicit exclusions from paying for direct clinical services or staff hours already billed to OAFC.

## OJJDP Skin [NEEDED: exact OJJDP solicitation]

OJJDP funding will be used to expand CCRB's juvenile justice impact and federal-grade accountability by supporting solicitation-aligned diversion and court-to-community continuity components, and by financing the additional evaluation and federal reporting workload required by DOJ/OJP systems. This lane will fund the implementation and measurement infrastructure necessary for JustGrants progress/performance reporting and SF-425-structured financial reporting, with timelines and deliverables aligned to OJJDP's semiannual reporting schedule. OJJDP dollars will be tracked under separate cost objectives with timekeeping rules consistent with 2 CFR 200 documentation standards, and will include explicit exclusions preventing OAFC-funded service delivery hours, naloxone procurement/distribution, and participant supports from being charged to the federal award unless a documented prorated allocation method is approved and supported by records.

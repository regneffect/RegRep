## Dual-County Design Rationale

**Harris County (Urban)**: High-volume juvenile court system, multiple CUP sites, established HCJPD relationship, CMU already delivering at Leadership Detention Center and CUP sites 1-4 and 8, CASE for Kids Opportunity Center presence. Full in-person service delivery capacity.

**Austin County (Rural)**: 2-person juvenile probation office with zero clinical capacity. Youth identified through Austin County JP are served via SRS telehealth platform for MOUD assessment, behavioral health screening, and tele-recovery supports. CMU provides virtual Credible Messenger mentoring with periodic in-person contacts. Demonstrates scalability of the model to underserved rural jurisdictions.

## OJJDP JDTC Guidelines Framework

CCRB aligns with the OJJDP Juvenile Drug Treatment Court (JDTC) Guidelines — 7 objectives and 28 implementation guidelines that structure evidence-based juvenile drug treatment court operations. Key alignments:

1. **Avoid Net Widening** — Target moderate-to-high-risk youth only, using validated screening
2. **Perform Comprehensive Assessments** — CRAFFT 2.1+N, RCAM, validated risk/needs tools
3. **Provide Comprehensive, Integrated Treatment** — MAT + recovery coaching + family engagement + education
4. **Engage Families** — CFP model with home visits, parent groups, benefits navigation
5. **Monitor and Track Outcomes** — Four-layer data architecture (JIMS2, CMU Tracker, UTHealth, Crosswalk)
6. **Build a Multidisciplinary Team** — Consortium model (GSI + CMU + SRS + UTHealth + HCJPD)
7. **Ensure Sustainability** — Braided funding architecture, credential stacking, workforce pipeline

## Core Workflow: 90-Day Stabilization + 12-Month Continuing Care

### Days 1-7: Intake & Assessment
- Universal SUD screening (CRAFFT 2.1+N)
- OUD risk assessment and stratification
- Educational status review + literacy screening
- Family engagement assessment
- Recovery Capital Assessment (RCAM, youth-adapted) — baseline
- Ages 16+ with suspected OUD: referred for MOUD clinical assessment via SRS telehealth (14-day linkage target)

### Days 8-30: Stabilization
- Weekly contact cadence; individualized recovery plan
- School reentry/GED coordination
- Family education on fentanyl/naloxone/MOUD
- SCBF 2.0 curriculum begins (Community-Based, School-Based, or After-School edition)
- Naloxone distributed to youth and family members
- Contingency management protocols activated

### Days 31-60: Active Recovery Navigation
- Vocational exploration, prosocial activities, mutual support
- Home visits (Certified Family Partner)
- 30-day milestone assessment
- MOUD adherence monitoring for clinical-track youth
- A-CRA-informed supports (SRS)

### Days 61-90: Transition Planning
- Long-term education placement; employment readiness
- Community recovery support connections
- 90-day completion assessment
- Warm handoffs to continuing care providers
- Outcome documentation for all funders

### Months 4-15: Youth Recovery Community (YRC)
- APG-informed continuing care for up to 12 months post-stabilization
- Decreasing intensity contact schedule
- Crisis re-engagement capacity
- Recovery capital reassessment (RCAM follow-up)
- Workforce pathway exploration (RSPS apprenticeship at 18+)

- **P**lanning — individualized recovery plan development
- **A**ssessment — ongoing progress monitoring
- **U**nderstanding — cultural context and lived experience integration
- **S**upport — resource mobilization and connection
- **E**valuation — outcome measurement and program improvement

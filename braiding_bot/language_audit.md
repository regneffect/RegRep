# Language Audit: Federal Language Environment Compliance

> **Last Updated**: March 7, 2026
> **Purpose**: Flag document scanning all grant narratives and external documents for terms that are problematic in the current federal funding environment. Each flagged term includes the risk, context, and approved replacement.

---

## Federal Language Environment Context (as of March 2026)

1. **SAMHSA** issued new strategic priorities in September 2025 explicitly deprioritizing "harm reduction" and DEI language in grant applications
2. **OJP FY2025** awards require civil rights certification that the recipient "does not operate any programs (including any such programs having components relating to diversity, equity, and inclusion) that violate any applicable Federal civil rights or nondiscrimination laws"
3. **OJJDP NOFO** (O-OJJDP-2025-172511) includes Agency Funding Priorities emphasizing: law enforcement support, combatting violent crime, protecting American children, supporting American citizens
4. **OJJDP NOFO** unallowable uses include programs that "unlawfully favor individuals in any race or protected group, including on a majority or minority, or privileged or unprivileged, basis"

## Flagged Terms and Approved Replacements

### HIGH RISK — Replace in All Federal Documents

| Flagged Term | Risk Level | Context | Approved Replacement | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **"harm reduction"** | HIGH | SAMHSA September 2025 strategic priorities explicitly deprioritized this term | **"recovery-oriented relapse response"** or **"overdose prevention"** or **"recovery-supportive risk mitigation"** | Naloxone distribution and fentanyl education are still fundable activities — the language framing has changed, not the services |
| **"diversity, equity, and inclusion" (DEI)** | HIGH | OJP FY2025 civil rights certification; executive orders | **Remove entirely** or use **"cultural competence"** (still a SAMHSA SPF guiding principle) | Do not use "DEI" in any form in federal applications |
| **"equity"** (standalone) | HIGH | Flagged when used as a standalone program goal or design principle | **"fairness"** or **"equal access"** or **"community-responsive service delivery"** | Acceptable in financial/budget contexts (e.g., "equity investment") |
| **"equitable"** | HIGH | Same concern as "equity" | **"accessible"** or **"responsive"** or **"community-appropriate"** | |
| **"inclusion" / "inclusive"** | MEDIUM | Risk when paired with identity-group language | **"comprehensive"** or **"community-wide"** or **"accessible to all eligible participants"** | Lower risk as standalone adjective |

### MEDIUM RISK — Review and Reframe

| Flagged Term | Risk Level | Context | Approved Replacement | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **"social justice"** | MEDIUM | Can be read as political advocacy | **"community well-being"** or **"public safety improvement"** | Reframe around outcomes, not ideology |
| **"systemic racism" / "structural racism"** | MEDIUM | Avoid in federal applications | **"documented disparities in justice system contact rates"** or **"community-level risk factors"** | Use data-driven language, not structural critique |
| **"marginalized"** | MEDIUM | Identity-framing language | **"underserved"** or **"historically underresourced"** or **"high-need"** | Focus on service gaps, not identity categories |
| **"lived experience"** (as sole qualification) | MEDIUM | Can be read as lowering professional standards | **"community-embedded operational credibility"** or **"practice expertise informed by personal recovery experience and professional certification"** | Always pair with credential/training language |
| **"housing first"** | MEDIUM | Deprioritized in SAMHSA-adjacent contexts | **"housing-linked recovery support"** or **"stable housing as recovery infrastructure"** | Frame housing as part of recovery capital, not as an ideology |
| **"trauma-informed"** | LOW-MEDIUM | Still widely accepted but increasingly scrutinized | **"evidence-based, developmentally appropriate practice"** or keep "trauma-informed" with clinical citation | Safer when paired with specific clinical frameworks |
| **"restorative justice"** | LOW | Generally accepted in OJJDP context | **"restorative accountability practices"** or **"community-based accountability and repair"** | OJJDP NOFO references "graduated, community-based sanctions" — align with this language |

### SAFE TERMS — Use Freely

| Term | Why It's Safe | Best Context |
|---|---|---|
| **"cultural competence"** | Still a SAMHSA SPF guiding principle | Workforce training, service delivery design |
| **"evidence-based"** | Core federal grant language | Program design, methodology |
| **"community-embedded credibility"** | Describes CM model without identity politics | CMU workforce description |
| **"recovery-oriented"** | Aligns with SAMHSA recovery framework | All service descriptions |
| **"protecting American children"** | Directly from OJJDP Agency Funding Priorities | OJJDP narrative — use prominently |
| **"public safety"** | Core DOJ mission language | OJJDP narrative framing |
| **"combatting violent crime"** | Directly from OJJDP Agency Funding Priorities | Violence interruption components |
| **"supporting services to American citizens"** | Directly from OJJDP Agency Funding Priorities | Service delivery description |
| **"recidivism reduction"** | Core OJJDP outcome measure | All OJJDP sections |
| **"validated assessment tools"** | Required by OJJDP Cat 2 | Screening methodology |
| **"community-based"** | In the name of Category 2 | Throughout OJJDP narrative |
| **"faith-based"** | Referenced in NOFO as eligible partner type | If relevant to consortium description |

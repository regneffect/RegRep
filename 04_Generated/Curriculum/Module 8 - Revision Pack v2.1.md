## 1. Strengthen the measurement (the program's summative layer)

- **Validate & align the pre/post survey:** confirm it measures the program's actual targets — fentanyl/2mg knowledge, **refusal self-efficacy**, coping-skill use, and intentions. Use validated scales where possible. Make sure each program objective has a matching survey item.
- **Add a 6-month follow-up survey** (mail it *with* the Letter to Future Me): a few behavioral/intention items = real **longitudinal outcome data** for funders.
- **Plan to actually score it:** pre → post → 6-month. Define your success metric *now* (e.g., % improvement in refusal self-efficacy).

## 2. Reframe the pledge (consistent with Module 1)

> **Old:** "You're signing a **contract** with your Future Me."
> **New:** "You're making a **promise to your Future Me** — you hold it, nobody else." Offer a **private signing** option for anyone who doesn't want to sign in front of the group. *(removes the coercion/surveillance feel for justice-impacted youth)*

## 3. Learning Objectives — ADD to "Facilitator Overview" (closure-appropriate)

By the end of Module 8, participants will be able to:
1. **Complete** the post-program assessment honestly. *(Apply)*
2. **Name** the tool from the Toolbox they'll rely on most and why. *(Understand)*
3. **Articulate** their commitment to their Future Me (the letter). *(Create)*

## 4. Non-shaming graduation note — ADD

> **Facilitator note:** "Everyone who showed up and did the work graduates. Being an Overcomer isn't about a perfect record — it's about having the tools and choosing to use them going forward. No one's certificate is conditional on never having slipped."

- The **post-survey** is the summative check.

## A1. Module 7 — Good Samaritan correction (final text)

> **Replace** the line claiming the law "is there to protect you... not run from the law" with:
>
> "Texas has a Good Samaritan law that **can** offer **some** protection if you call 911 in good faith for an overdose — but it's **limited, has conditions, and gets more complicated if you're on probation or parole.** It is **not** a free pass. # PART B — P1: The Measurement System (funder-critical)

## B1. Objectives + Exit Tickets — all 8 modules

| Module | Learning objectives (by end, youth can…) | Exit ticket (≤3 items) |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Define Future Me + a value; write a SMART goal w/ deadline; explain gas-pedal/brakes; commit to a next step | Future Me in 1 word; your SMART goal; one thing that could pull you off track |
| **2** | Recognize 2mg is lethal & fakes look real; ID 3 digital red flags; describe OD signs + 2 responses; name a protective strategy | 2 red flags; what to do if a friend ODs; one offline strategy |
| **3** | Explain gas/brakes; describe the dopamine hijack; perform the 5-Second Override; commit to using it | Name the 5 Override steps; one situation to use it; gas or brakes runs your day? |
| **4** | Recall the 6 STRONG steps; demonstrate an assertive refusal; choose a strategy incl. when to exit; ID a Healthy High | List STRONG; your go-to refusal line; one time leaving/Block is safest |
| **5** | ID personal triggers; distinguish healthy vs fake high; perform 5-4-3-2-1; build a coping menu (≥3 specific) | Top 2 menu choices; one trigger to watch; healthy vs fake in one line |
| **6** | Recall SPAUSE; apply it to a scenario; predict ripple consequences; connect to STRONG at "Exit" | List SPAUSE; one ripple of a good choice; where your pocket card lives |
| **7** | ID 2 safe people + 2 safe places; recognize OD signs; demonstrate Narcan+911 response; set an accountability partner | One safe person + place; 2 OD signs; your partner's name |
| **8** | Complete the post-assessment; name the top tool they'll use + why; articulate their commitment | Name ≥1 tool + when you'll use it; (post-survey is the summative check) |

**Mastery rules (formative):** SMART goal complete (S+M+deadline) = M1; ≥3 correct red flags = M2; recite 5 Override steps = M3; refusal hits state-no/stance/exit = M4; menu has ≥3 specific items = M5; walks all 6 SPAUSE steps = M6; states OD signs + 4 response steps = M7.

## B2. Aligned Pre/Post Survey (draft — identical pre & post)

**Knowledge (T/F or MC):**
1. As little as 2mg of fentanyl can be deadly. *(T)*
2. You can usually tell a fake pill from a real one by looking. *(F — reverse-scored)*
3. Naloxone (Narcan) can reverse an opioid overdose. *(T)*
4. Slow/stopped breathing and blue lips are overdose signs. *(T)*
5. Which is a red flag of a digital "plug"? *(MC)*

**Refusal self-efficacy (1 = not at all → 5 = totally confident):**
6. I can say no to a pill a friend offers.
7. I can leave a situation that feels risky.
8. I know what to say when someone pressures me.

**Coping / regulation (1–5 agree):**
9. When I'm stressed, I have healthy ways to cope.
10. I can calm myself down when I'm overwhelmed.
11. I can pause before a risky decision.

**Intentions / future orientation (1–5 agree):**
12. I have a clear goal for my future.
13. I intend to avoid fake/counterfeit pills.
14. I have people I can go to when things get hard.

**Post-only feedback:** 15. The program gave me tools I'll use (1–5). 16. It felt safe and real (1–5). 17. One tool I'm taking with me: ____.

## B3. 6-Month Follow-Up Survey (draft — short)

1. Since the program, which tools have you used? (Override / grounding / SPAUSE / STRONG / coping menu / none)
2. Repeat self-efficacy items 6–8 (1–5).
3. Describe one time you used a tool. (open)
4. Do you still have your safety network and coping menu? (Y/N)

## B4. Scoring & Success Metrics

- **Knowledge:** % correct, pre → post. *Target: +20 points or ≥80% post.*
- **Refusal self-efficacy:** mean (items 6–8), pre → post → 6-mo. *Target: ≥+1.0 increase, maintained at 6-mo.*
- **Coping / intentions:** mean change pre → post.
- **Formative:** % of youth hitting each module's mastery rule (B1).
- **Report:** pre → post change + 6-month maintenance per cohort. Define your headline metric before the next cohort (recommend: refusal self-efficacy gain + % completing a SMART goal).

## 1. Learning Objectives — ADD to "Facilitator Overview"

By the end of Module 3, participants will be able to:
1. **Explain** the "gas pedal vs. brakes" model and why the teen brain favors emotion over logic. *(Understand)*
2. **Describe** how substances hijack the brain's dopamine system. *(Understand)*
3. **Perform** the 5 steps of the 5-Second Override (Freeze, Breathe, Label, Visualize, Decide). *(Apply)*
4. **Commit** to using the Override on one real urge before the next session. *(Apply)*

## 2. Formalize the Skill Check + Exit Ticket

- **Skill check (in Activity 4):** each youth demonstrates or recites the **5 steps** unprompted = mastery of Objective 3. Track % who can.
- **Exit ticket (~3 min):** (1) Name the 5 Override steps; (2) One situation this week you'll use it; (3) "Gas pedal or brakes — which one runs your day more?" → measures Objectives 1, 3, 4.

## 3. Make the Skill Stick — ADD a generalization plan (the key upgrade)

Single practice won't transfer. Add:
- **Scenario reps (in Activity 4):** after the first practice, run the Override against 2–3 quick prompts ("a Plug DMs you," "someone hands you a pill at a party," "you're furious at a teacher") — *transfer beats repetition-in-a-vacuum.*
- **Between-session prompt:** "Catch yourself pumping the brakes once this week — what was the urge?" Collect at the next module's open.
- **Module 4 opener callback:** start M4 with a 60-second "Brakes Check-In" — who used it, what happened. This builds the rehearsal loop the skill needs.

## 4. Trauma-Informed Note — ADD to Activity 4 ("Label the feeling")

> **Facilitator note:** "Naming a feeling is the skill — *'name it to tame it.'* Some names that come up are big (grief, rage). You don't have to say yours out loud; labeling it in your own head still works. If something heavy comes up, you can talk to me after." *(holds space; no forced sharing)*

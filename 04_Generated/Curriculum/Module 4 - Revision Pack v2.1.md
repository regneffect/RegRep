## 2. Rebalance the refusal coaching — REPLACE the "mom" note

> **Old:** "Don't blame your mom… makes you look weak."
> **New facilitator coaching:** "There's no one 'right' refusal — you want a **menu** so you've always got one ready. Owning it ('I'm staying sharp for my Blueprint') is powerful. But a quick external out ('my coach drug-tests us') is also a real tool — use whatever keeps you safe and out. The only wrong move is freezing." *(offers multiple validated strategies; removes the shaming "weak" frame)*

## 3. Add the safety caveat — NEW facilitator note in Activity 3

> **Facilitator note (safety):** "The assertive stand is for everyday pressure. But read the room — if a situation is truly dangerous or someone's looking for a reason to escalate, the strongest, smartest refusal is to **leave quietly or hit Block**, not to win a standoff. Safety beats looking solid every time."

## 4. Learning Objectives — ADD to "Facilitator Overview"

By the end of Module 4, participants will be able to:
1. **Recall** the 6 steps of the S.T.R.O.N.G. method. *(Understand)*
2. **Demonstrate** an assertive refusal (verbal + posture) in a role-play. *(Apply)*
3. **Choose** an appropriate refusal strategy for the situation, including when to simply exit. *(Apply/Analyze)*
4. **Identify** one Healthy High to redirect to. *(Apply)*

## 5. Role-Play Rubric + Exit Ticket (the measurement layer)

- **Role-play check (Activity 4):** score each youth's refusal on 3 things — *(1) stated a clear no, (2) used the assertive stand, (3) got moving / exited.* 2 of 3 = mastery of Objective 2.
- **Exit ticket (~3 min):** (1) List the S.T.R.O.N.G. steps; (2) Your go-to refusal line; (3) One situation where leaving/Block is the safest call. → Objectives 1, 3, 4.

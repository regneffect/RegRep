## 1. 🔧 FIX — Good Samaritan Law (accuracy + safety; do this first)

> **Replace** "The Good Samaritan Law is there to protect you so you can focus on saving a life, not running from the law."
>
> **New facilitator script (honest version):** "Texas has a Good Samaritan law that *can* offer **some** protection if you call 911 for an overdose in good faith — but it's **limited and has conditions**. It is **not** a free pass, and if you're on probation or parole it gets more complicated. So here's the real deal: know that the protection exists but isn't guaranteed — **and a life is still worth more than a charge.** If someone's going down: give Narcan, call 911, stay. We'll get you the exact current rules."
>
> ⚠️ **Facilitator MUST do before teaching:** pull the **current Texas Good Samaritan statute** and confirm exactly what it does/doesn't cover, **including how probation/parole status affects it.** Do not promise blanket immunity. Localize it. *(This is a justice-impacted population — getting this wrong can cause real harm.)*

## 4. Learning Objectives — ADD to "Facilitator Overview"

By the end of Module 7, participants will be able to:
1. **Identify** 2 Safe People and 2 Safe Places in their life. *(Apply)*
2. **Recognize** the signs of an opioid overdose. *(Understand)*
3. **Demonstrate / describe** how to respond: Narcan, call 911, stay, rescue position. *(Apply)*
4. **Establish** an accountability check-in partner and plan. *(Apply)*

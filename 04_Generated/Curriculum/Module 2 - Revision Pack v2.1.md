## ⚠️ 1. Overdose-Response / Harm-Reduction — ADD a segment (the priority fix)

> **Program decision flag:** whether to teach harm reduction is yours/the funder's call. But naloxone awareness + "call 911" is **life-safety, not ideology** — recommended at minimum. Insert ~8–10 min before the closing.

**New Activity 4.5 — "How to Keep Your People Alive" (~8 min)**
> **Facilitator script (suggested):** "Knowing the trap is half of it. The other half: what do you do if someone *does* take a pill and goes down? You don't panic — you act."
> - **Signs of an overdose:** won't wake up, slow/no breathing, blue/gray lips or fingertips, gurgling/snoring sound.
> - **Naloxone (Narcan):** a nasal spray that reverses an opioid overdose. It's free/low-cost in Texas (pharmacy standing order; community programs). It does nothing harmful if you're wrong — so if in doubt, use it.
> - **Call 911.** Say "someone isn't breathing." Stay until help comes.
> - **Never use alone** — most fatal overdoses happen when no one's there to call for help.
> ⚠️ **Verify before teaching:** current Texas naloxone access points, and **Texas Good Samaritan law specifics** (TX protection is limited — confirm exactly what it covers before promising immunity).

## 2. Trauma-Informed Scaffolding — ADD facilitator notes

### Open of Activity 2 (before "death sentence" language)
> **Facilitator note:** "Before we get into this — some of us have already lost somebody to this. If that's you, this might hit different. You can step out for water anytime, no questions. We're not doing this to scare you; we're doing it so you've got the same information that could've changed things." *(acknowledges loss; opt-out; reframes from fear to power)*

### Throughout
> Lead with **skills and protection**, not body counts. Research shows fear-based messaging alone is weak — pair every scary fact with an action the youth can take.

## 3. Learning Objectives — ADD to "Facilitator Overview"

By the end of Module 2, participants will be able to:
1. **Recognize** that 2mg of fentanyl can be lethal and that counterfeit pills are visually indistinguishable from real ones. *(Understand)*
2. **Identify** at least 3 red flags of digital drug selling. *(Analyze)*
3. **Describe** the signs of an opioid overdose and at least 2 response actions (naloxone, call 911). *(Understand/Apply)*
4. **Name** one personal protective strategy ("offline strategy"). *(Apply)*

## 4. Formalize the Check + Exit Ticket

- **"Spot the Plug"** already works as a formative check — **score it:** groups that correctly name ≥3 red flags = mastery of Objective 2.
- **Exit ticket (~3 min):** (1) Two red flags of a digital plug; (2) One thing you'd do if a friend overdosed; (3) Your one offline strategy. → measures Objectives 2, 3, 4.

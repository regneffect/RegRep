## 1. Learning Objectives — ADD to "Facilitator Overview"

By the end of Module 5, participants will be able to:
1. **Identify** their personal stress/boredom triggers for seeking a high. *(Understand)*
2. **Distinguish** a "Healthy High" from a "Fake High" and explain the difference. *(Understand)*
3. **Perform** the 5-4-3-2-1 grounding exercise. *(Apply)*
4. **Create** a personal Coping Menu with at least 3 realistic, specific options. *(Create)*

## 2. Formalize the measure — the Coping Menu IS your check

- **Coping Menu rubric:** a completed menu with **≥3 specific, personalized, realistic** options = mastery of Objective 4. (Reject vague entries like "chill" — push for "shoot 50 free throws," "make one beat.")
- **Grounding check:** youth can name and perform the 5-4-3-2-1 steps = Objective 3.
- **Exit ticket (~3 min):** (1) Your top 2 Coping Menu choices; (2) One trigger you'll watch for; (3) Healthy vs. Fake high — one-line difference. → Objectives 1, 2, 4.

## 3. Add the rehearsal loop (so the tools get used)

- **Between-session prompt:** "Pull one thing off your Coping Menu before next session — which one, and did it help?"
- **Module 6 opener:** 60-second "Menu Check" — who used their menu or grounded, what happened. *(Same loop pattern as Module 3/4 — this is what builds transfer.)*

## 4. Trauma-Informed Note — ADD to Activity 3 (grounding)

> **Facilitator note:** "For most people 5-4-3-2-1 calms things down. For a few, slowing down can let big feelings surface — that's normal. If that's you, open your eyes, plant your feet, and you can check in with me after. You're in control of how far you go." *(normalizes; opt-out; no forcing)*

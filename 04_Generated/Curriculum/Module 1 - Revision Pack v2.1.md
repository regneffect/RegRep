## 1. Learning Objectives — ADD to "Facilitator Overview"

By the end of Module 1, participants will be able to:

1. **Define** their "Future Me" and name at least one core value driving it. *(Bloom: Understand)*
2. **Write** one S.M.A.R.T. goal with a specific deadline. *(Apply / Create)*
3. **Explain**, in their own words, the "gas pedal vs. brakes" model of the teen brain. *(Understand)*
4. **Commit** to one concrete next step toward their goal. *(Apply)*

## 2. Exit Ticket — ADD as the module-level check (~3 min, end of class)

**"Future Me Check"** — index card or back of Handout R:
- Your Future Me in **ONE word**: ______
- My **S.M.A.R.T. goal**: ______
- One thing that could **pull me off track**: ______

Facilitator collects them. **This is your Module 1 data** (pairs with the program pre-survey).
**Measurement rule:** count the % of cards with a *complete* SMART goal (specific + measurable + deadline) = mastery indicator for Objective 2. Counts toward Objectives 1 and 4 too.

## 3. Trauma-Informed Scaffolding — ADD as facilitator notes

### Activity 2 — "Meet Your Future Me" (insert *before* the visualization)
> **Facilitator note (trauma-informed):**
> - "You can keep your eyes open if that's more comfortable." *(opt-out)*
> - Grounding alternative: "If picturing 5 years feels like too much, just picture **next Friday** — a good version of next week." *(scaffolds youth who can't envision far futures)*
> - If a youth disengages, normalize it: "Some of us have had people taken out of that picture. That's real. You can picture the future you'd **protect for someone else** if that's easier." *(holds space for grief/loss without forcing)*
> - **Never force sharing.**

### Activity 5 — "Staying Strong Pledge" (reframe)
> **Reframe from "contract" → a promise to yourself:**
> - "This isn't a contract with me, or the court. It's a promise to that **Future Me**. You hold it — not me."
> - Make it voluntary: "Not ready to sign? Fold it and keep it. The promise still counts when you're ready."
> - Let them sign with **initials or a symbol**, not a full legal name. *(removes the surveillance feel for justice-impacted youth)*

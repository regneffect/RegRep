# PART D — P2: Trauma-Informed Consistency Pass

## D1. The SCBF Trauma-Informed Note Standard (one template, applied everywhere heavy)

Use this 5-part standard anywhere a module touches loss, danger, pressure, or past choices:

1. **Name it** — "This topic may hit hard; some of us have lived it."
2. **Opt-out** — "You can step out, keep your eyes open, or pass. No questions."
3. **No forced sharing** — "Participation is voluntary; you can always use a hypothetical instead of your own story."
4. **Distress response (facilitator)** — don't probe; validate ("that took guts"); return to the skill; check in **privately** after.
5. **Reporting awareness (facilitator)** — know your mandatory-reporting obligations **before** sensitive modules.

**Where to place it:**

| Module | Heavy moment | Note focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Future Me" visualization; pledge | loss / can't-envision; voluntary |
| 2 | Fentanyl death content | loss acknowledgment |
| 3 | "Label the feeling" | big emotions surfacing |
| 4 | Refusal role-play | pressure activation + safety caveat |
| 5 | 5-4-3-2-1 grounding | possible increased activation |
| 6 | Decision Lab | revisiting real choices |
| 7 | Overdose discussion + Narcan | loss; reporting awareness |
| 8 | Pledge / graduation | non-shaming for those who struggled |

## D2. Module 6 — Decision Lab redesign (final text)

> **New facilitator script:** "We're going to put S.P.A.U.S.E. to work — your call how. Use **one of these scenario cards**, OR something you've seen or heard about, OR something from your own life. You don't have to share details, and you don't have to share at all. We're not digging up regrets — we're building the move you'll make **next time**."
> - Always offer the **scenario-card / hypothetical** option.
> - **Forward frame:** "What's your S.P.A.U.S.E. play *next time*?" (not "what went wrong").
> - Or surface a **win:** "Tell us about a time you already paused and protected your Blueprint."
> - Apply the **D1 distress + reporting notes.**

## D3. Pledge reframes (Modules 1 & 8 — final text)

> **Replace "contract" framing with:** "This is a **promise to your Future Me** — you hold it, nobody else. Not ready to sign in front of everyone? You can sign privately and keep it. The promise still counts." *(removes coercion/surveillance feel; offers a private option)*

## D4. Module 4 — safety caveat (final text)

> **Facilitator note (Activity 3):** "The assertive stand is for everyday pressure. But read the room — if a situation is truly dangerous or someone's looking to escalate, the smartest refusal is to **leave quietly or hit Block**, not win a standoff. Safety beats looking solid, every time."

## E2. "How My Tools Connect" — one-pager (give to every participant)

**THE SCBF TOOLBOX — when to use what**

- **Your foundation:** the Future Me Blueprint + SMART goal *(M1)* — your *why*.
- **When an urge or pressure hits:**
  1. **Buy time** → 5-Second Override *(M3)*: Freeze · Breathe · Label · Visualize · Decide. *(Overwhelmed or angry? Use 5-4-3-2-1 grounding (M5) instead.)*
  2. **Decide** → S.P.A.U.S.E. *(M6)*: Stop · Predict · Assess · Understand · Select · Exit.
  3. **Get out** → S.T.R.O.N.G. *(M4)*: State · Tell · Re-brand · Offline · No-negotiation · Get-moving.
  4. **Refill** → your Coping Menu / Healthy High *(M5)*.
- **Your backup:** the Pack — safe people + safe places + your accountability partner *(M7)*.
- **Emergency:** overdose signs → **Narcan + 911 + stay** *(M2/M7)*.

## E3. Skill-generalization loops (so the tools transfer)

- **M3 (Override):** run 2–3 scenario reps in-session; between-session "catch yourself pumping the brakes" prompt.
- **M5 (grounding + menu):** practice grounding twice; "pull one menu item this week."
- **Program-wide:** add a standing **60-second "Tool Check-In"** opener to Modules 4–8 — *who used a tool, what happened.* This single habit builds the rehearsal loop the skills need.

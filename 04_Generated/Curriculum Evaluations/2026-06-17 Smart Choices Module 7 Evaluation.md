## Verdict: ✅ REVISE — one defect is a safety/accuracy issue, fix first

This module carries the program's most important life-safety content (Narcan). That makes the legal error more serious, not less.

## Top gaps (highest impact first)

1. **🔧 OVERSTATES the Good Samaritan Law (accuracy + safety).** The script says the law "is there to protect you so you can focus on saving a life, not running from the law." **Texas's overdose Good Samaritan protection is limited and conditional** — it is *not* blanket immunity, and **probation/parole status complicates it.** Telling justice-impacted youth they're "protected" could get someone hurt. Must be corrected to accurate, localized info. *(dimensions 3, 6)*
2. **🔗 Sequencing vs. Module 2.** This is the overdose/Narcan content M2 was missing — but it lands **5 weeks after** the "one pill kills" module. A youth could face a crisis in between. Front-load *basic* naloxone+911 awareness in M2; keep the full training here. *(curriculum arc)*
3. **Narcan may need more than discussion.** 20 min of discussion for an actual life-saving skill is light — make it a **practiced demo** with the trainer kit. *(rigor/outcomes)*
4. **No measurable objectives / formalized checks.** *(dimensions 1, 9)*

## Red-team — where it fails in a real room

- A youth on probation calls 911 expecting the promised "protection," gets charged or violated, and the curriculum **told them they were safe.** This is the highest-stakes error in the program.
- Overdose signs discussed with a youth who **lost someone that way** — no loss-acknowledgment built in.
- Accountability partner relapses or ghosts; youth was taught to rely on a peer as the crisis plan with no adult/professional backstop.

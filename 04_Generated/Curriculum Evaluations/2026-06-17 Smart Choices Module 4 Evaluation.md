## Verdict: ✅ REVISE — fix the defect first

Strong, evidence-aligned skills module — but it can't ship while its **central acronym is defined two ways.**

## Top gaps (highest impact first)

1. **🔧 DEFECT — S.T.R.O.N.G. is inconsistent.** Script: "State, Tell, Redirect, Get moving" (4 letters). Slide: "State, Tell, Re-brand, Offline, No excuses, Get moving" (6). A youth can't learn a 6-letter method taught two ways. **Lock one definition everywhere.** *(dimensions 1, 10)*
2. **Debatable coaching: "don't blame your mom… makes you look weak."** Best practice gives youth a **menu** of refusal strategies — including a low-stakes external "out." Don't eliminate a valid tool, and drop the shaming "weak" frame. *(dimensions 3, 5)*
3. **Safety caveat missing.** A status-standoff "assertive stand" can escalate in real high-risk contexts. Add: sometimes the safest refusal is a **quiet exit / the Block button**, not a confrontation. *(dimension 6)*
4. **No measurable objectives or formalized role-play rubric.** *(dimensions 1, 9)*
5. **Cleanup:** the source doc ends with a stray line — *"Would you like the revised Module 5…next?"* — an AI-generation artifact. Remove it.

## Red-team — where it fails in a real room

- Two facilitators teach two different S.T.R.O.N.G.s → kids compare notes, trust drops.
- A youth in a genuinely dangerous situation uses the "assertive stand," it's read as disrespect, and it **escalates** — the curriculum gave them confrontation without a safety filter.
- A kid who relies on "my mom would kill me" (a real, effective out) is told that's "weak" — loses their working strategy.

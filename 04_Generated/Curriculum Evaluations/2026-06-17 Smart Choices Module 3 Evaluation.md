## Verdict: ✅ REVISE (light) — closest to Ready of Modules 1–3

This module does what good prevention should: teaches a **transferable skill**, removes shame, and stays evidence-aligned. It mostly just needs the **measurement layer** and a **skill-generalization plan**.

## Top gaps (highest impact first)

1. **No measurable objectives or formalized skill check.** Can a youth *perform* and *recall* the 5 steps? Make that the measure. *(dimensions 1, 9)*
2. **One-and-done skill practice.** The Override is practiced once. **Single-session skills rarely transfer** — it needs rehearsal across modules + a between-session "catch yourself" prompt. *(rigor/outcomes)*
3. **Minor trauma note:** "Label the feeling" can open big emotions — add a hold-space, no-forced-sharing note. *(dimension 6)*
4. **Evidence uncited.** *(dimension 3)*

## Red-team — where it fails in a real room

- The skill feels great in a calm room and **vanishes under real pressure** — without rehearsal + transfer cues, it won't generalize to the moment a Plug hits the DMs.
- "Overcomer vs. a statistic" is a small slip back toward labeling/fear — fine, but the rest is empowering; keep the empowerment dominant.
- A youth labels a feeling that's bigger than expected (grief, rage) and the facilitator has no scripted way to hold it.

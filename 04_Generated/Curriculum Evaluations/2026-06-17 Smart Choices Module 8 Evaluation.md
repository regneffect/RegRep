## Verdict: ✅ REVISE (light) — strong closer

A genuinely good finale: consolidation + celebration + a commitment device (the mailed letter) that doubles as a booster. The upgrades are about **measurement quality** and one consistent reframe.

## Top gaps (highest impact first)

1. **Validate & align the pre/post survey.** It's your summative measure — confirm it's a **validated instrument** that maps to the program's actual objectives (fentanyl knowledge, refusal self-efficacy, coping use, intentions). *(dimension 9)*
2. **Add a 6-month follow-up survey** (alongside the mailed letter) for real **longitudinal outcomes** — funder gold. *(dimension 9)*
3. **Reframe the pledge** from "signing a **contract**" to a voluntary **promise to self**, with an opt-out for the public signing. (Consistent with the Module 1 finding.) *(dimension 6)*
4. **Evidence uncited.** *(dimension 3)*

## Red-team — where it fails in a real room

- A youth who struggled all program feels like a fraud "graduating" — the "Overcomer" frame mostly prevents this; make sure no one's graduation is conditional on having been perfect.
- A kid doesn't want to sign/perform publicly and feels singled out — needs a private option.
- The post-survey is collected but **never analyzed** — without a plan to score pre→post, it's data you don't use.

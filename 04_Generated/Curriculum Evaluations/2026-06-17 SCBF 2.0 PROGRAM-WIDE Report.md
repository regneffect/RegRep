## Executive summary

SCBF 2.0 is a **pedagogically strong, culturally grounded, evidence-aligned** prevention curriculum with a genuine cumulative skill arc (SMART → STRONG → Override → grounding → SPAUSE → safety net → launch). Its writing, voice, and engagement are **above what most prevention curricula achieve.**

It is **not yet "ready to ship" as written** because of two safety/accuracy defects and one systemic gap:
- 🔴 **A legal overstatement (M7 Good Samaritan)** that could harm justice-impacted youth.
- 🔴 **A core method taught two ways (M4 S.T.R.O.N.G.).**
- 🟠 **No module-level measurement** anywhere (program-level pre/post exists; formative checks don't).

**Program average: 3.95 / 5.** With a focused revision (mostly the P0–P1 list below), the whole program lifts to **~4.5 = Ready to teach.**

## The patterns (read these, not just the scores)

1. **Measurement is the #1 systemic gap (2.4).** Every module *teaches* well but can't *prove* it worked. Program-level pre/post exists (M1 → M8) but the survey's validity/alignment is unconfirmed, there's no module-level formative check, and no longitudinal follow-up. **This is the single highest-leverage fix and it's funder-critical.**

2. **Trauma-informed scaffolding is inconsistent (3.4, range 2→5).** The curriculum handles heavy material — fentanyl death, loss, refusal under pressure, decision regret — but the trauma supports are excellent in M5 and thin in M2/M4/M6/M7. It needs **one consistent standard** applied across all modules.

3. **Two hard defects** undermine otherwise strong modules: the M4 acronym inconsistency and the M7 legal overstatement. Both are quick fixes with outsized impact (the legal one is a real safety issue for this population).

4. **One sequencing issue:** the "one pill kills" content (M2) and "what to do about it" (Narcan, M7) are **5 weeks apart.**

5. **Evidence is uncited throughout** (content quality ~3.9, but zero citations). The material is *already* evidence-aligned — it just needs to say so. Pure upside for funders/replication.

6. **The strengths are real and consistent — protect them.** Developmental fit (5.0), cultural responsiveness/strength-based (4.75), and engagement (4.75) are the curriculum's identity: the voice, the metaphors ("Ferrari with bicycle brakes," "Future Me," "the Pack"), the de-surveillance framing, and the cumulative skill arc. **Do not sanitize these in revision.**

## One prioritized fix list (whole program)

### 🔴 P0 — Safety & accuracy (before the next cohort)
1. **M7 — correct the Good Samaritan content.** Verify the current Texas statute; state honestly that protection is limited/conditional and that probation/parole complicates it. Never promise blanket immunity.
2. **M4 — unify the S.T.R.O.N.G. acronym** across script, slide, and Handout H (one definition; resolve "No excuses" vs "Tell your reason").
3. **M2 — front-load basic naloxone + 911 awareness** with the fentanyl content (full training stays in M7).

### 🟠 P1 — Make it measurable (program integrity / funder-critical)
4. **Add learning objectives + a short exit ticket to all 8 modules** (templates already drafted in each revision pack).
5. **Validate & align the pre/post survey** to the program's objectives; **add a 6-month follow-up survey.**
6. **Define the success metric and a scoring plan** (pre → post → follow-up).

### 🟡 P2 — Trauma-informed consistency pass
7. **Apply one trauma-informed note standard** across modules: acknowledge loss, opt-out, no forced sharing, distress response, mandatory-reporting awareness.
8. **Redesign the M6 Decision Lab** (voluntary, hypothetical option, forward frame).
9. **Reframe the pledges (M1, M8)** from "contract" → voluntary promise to self; offer a private signing option.
10. **Add the M4 safety caveat** (sometimes the safest refusal is to leave/Block).

### 🟢 P3 — Evidence & polish
11. **Add citations across all modules** (the content is already there — cite Steinberg/Casey, Volkow, Lieberman, Botvin, Hershfield, SAMHSA, etc.).
12. **"How My Tools Connect" one-pager** (reduce framework load: Brakes → Override/Grounding → SPAUSE → STRONG → Coping Menu).
13. **Skill-generalization loops** (M3, M5): between-session prompts + next-module check-ins.
14. **Cleanup:** remove the stray AI line in M4; standardize module filenames.

## Verdict: ✅ REVISE (light) — strong bones, a few high-leverage fixes. Not a rebuild.

This is genuinely good orientation material — the voice, cultural fit, and engagement are above what most prevention curricula achieve. The gaps are structural (measurement, objectives, trauma scaffolding), not content.

## Top gaps (highest impact first)

1. **No measurable learning objectives or module-level check.** You can't yet prove Module 1 did anything. *(hits dimensions 1, 9)*
2. **Visualization & pledge need trauma-informed scaffolding.** *(dimension 6)*
3. **No alignment map** (objective ↔ activity ↔ handout ↔ check). *(dimension 1)*
4. **Evidence base uncited** — weakens the funder/replication case. *(dimension 3)*

## Smallest high-leverage fixes

- **Write 4 objectives + 1 exit ticket** (~15 min). E.g., exit ticket: *"Your Future Me in one word + your SMART goal."* Now it's measurable.
- **Add a 2-line trauma-informed note** to Activity 2 (visualization) and Activity 5 (pledge): opt-out language, a grounding option, and a fallback for youth who can't picture a future.
- **Add citations** for the adolescent-brain content and SMART goals.

## Red-team — where it fails in a real room

- A youth who's **lost people or can't imagine 5 years out** shuts down during "Future Me." No fallback is given.
- The **pledge/"contract"** can read as coercive or surveillance-like to justice-impacted youth. Reframe as a **voluntary promise to self**, not a contract.
- **90 minutes is talk-heavy** (lots of facilitator monologue). Engagement may dip without more youth voice/movement between scripts.
- "No one's looking at your specific answers" mostly defuses survey distrust — keep that; it's well done.

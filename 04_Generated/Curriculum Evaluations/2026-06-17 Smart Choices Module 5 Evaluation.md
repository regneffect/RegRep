## Verdict: ✅ REVISE (light) — nearly Ready

Trauma-informed design and evidence base are exemplary here. This module mostly just needs the **measurement layer** the whole program is missing, plus a bit more skill rehearsal.

## Top gaps (highest impact first)

1. **No formal objectives / exit ticket** — though you're closest here: the **Coping Menu is already a measurable artifact.** Formalize it. *(dimensions 1, 9)*
2. **Two skills, practiced once each** (grounding + menu). Same generalization risk as Module 3 — needs a "use it before next session" loop. *(rigor/outcomes)*
3. **Minor trauma note:** grounding occasionally *increases* activation for some youth — add a brief "if that happens, here's what to do" facilitator note. *(dimension 6)*
4. **Evidence uncited.** *(dimension 3)*

## Red-team — where it fails in a real room

- The Coping Menu gets filled out and **never looked at again** — without a "pull your menu this week" loop, it's a worksheet, not a tool.
- A youth grounds and gets *more* anxious (happens occasionally) and the facilitator isn't prepped for it.
- "Gaming with the squad" as a Healthy High is fine — but worth a one-line nuance that anything can flip compulsive; moderation matters.

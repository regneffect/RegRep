## Verdict: ✅ REVISE — strong integrator, fix the Decision Lab

This is the capstone-style module and it does integration well. The one real risk is the **Decision Lab**.

## Top gaps (highest impact first)

1. **Decision Lab is a trauma risk as written.** Asking justice-impacted youth to **publicly revisit a real bad choice** and imagine "how would Future Me have changed that night" can induce shame/regret or surface trauma. Needs: voluntary, a hypothetical option, a **forward** reframe (not "what went wrong"), and a facilitator note for heavy disclosures. *(dimension 6)*
2. **No measurable objectives / formalized check.** *(dimensions 1, 9)*
3. **Evidence uncited.** *(dimension 3)*
4. **Framework load:** S.T.R.O.N.G. + S.P.A.U.S.E. + gas/brakes — a one-page "how my tools connect" would reduce cognitive load. *(minor)*

## Red-team — where it fails in a real room

- A youth shares a genuinely traumatic "that night," the room goes heavy, and the facilitator has no scripted way to hold it or close it safely.
- The regret framing ("what would Future Me have said to you then") lands as **"you messed up"** for a kid already carrying shame.
- Pocket card gets cut out and lost by next week — without a "where did you put yours?" check, it's craft time, not transfer.

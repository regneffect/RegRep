# Braided Funding Map: OAFC + Hogg + OJJDP

## Design Rule

**One spine, three lanes**: OAFC funds the opioid-abatement service lane; Hogg funds the mental-health capacity/infrastructure lane; OJJDP funds the juvenile justice expansion + federal reporting/evaluation lane. Each cost objective is assigned once (or split by a documented allocation method) and the same outputs are not claimed to multiple funders.

---

## OAFC (State) - "Direct CCRB Service Delivery for OUD/SUD Abatement"

### Pays For (Distinct Lane)

- MOUD linkage navigation operations tied to the 14-day assessment target (non-clinical navigation and coordination as described in CCRB)
- Evidence-based stabilization phase delivery supports (peer recovery support, contingency management operations, recovery coaching)
- Naloxone education/distribution operations + supplies (as described in OAFC package)
- Certified Family Partner service delivery for bystander education and family navigation
- Participant support costs explicitly budgeted/allowed in OAFC award (e.g., transport supports, stabilization supports) [VERIFY OAFC/NOFA cost rules at award]
- OAFC-required outcome tracking and quarterly reporting work that is specific to OAFC deliverables

### Does NOT Pay For

- Hogg-funded organizational capacity deliverables (training system build, QA system build, mental health infrastructure build)
- OJJDP-funded federal reporting overhead or OJJDP-specific evaluation deliverables
- Any cost already charged to another funder ("no duplicate benefit" rule)

## Grant Skin Paragraphs

### Universal Spine Paragraph

Court-Connected Recovery Bridge (CCRB) is a court-to-community recovery continuum serving transitional age youth (ages 12-24) with opioid/substance use involvement who are identified through Harris County juvenile and young adult courts and juvenile probation referral pathways. CCRB operates as a 90-day stabilization model followed by up to 12 months of structured continuing care through an APG-informed Youth Recovery Community (YRC). The program links court engagement to evidence-based recovery supports, including peer-led recovery coaching, contingency management, and adolescent-focused treatment elements (MET/CBT and A-CRA elements), with service delivery adapted for low literacy and reinforced through universal literacy screening at intake. For youth ages 16+ with suspected opioid use disorder, CCRB prioritizes rapid medication linkage through a telehealth-enabled MOUD assessment pathway with a 14-day target. CCRB equips families as safety infrastructure through Certified Family Partner navigation, fentanyl and naloxone education, and ongoing family stabilization support. Naloxone education and distribution are embedded as a harm-reduction layer to reduce overdose risk among youth and their households. CCRB is delivered through a consortium model in which Grace Solutions International serves as fiscal agent and compliance backbone, Credible Messengers United provides credible messenger peer operations and continuing care, and Strategic Recovery Solutions provides MOUD linkage navigation and Certified Family Partner services, with an external evaluation partner supporting outcome documentation. CCRB's performance targets include timely assessment, treatment initiation, stabilization completion, continuing care engagement, and naloxone distribution as measurable outputs.

### OAFC-Specific Skin

OAFC funding will be used to operate CCRB's opioid-abatement service lane for justice-involved transitional age youth by directly supporting the 90-day stabilization workflow and the 12-month Youth Recovery Community continuing-care structure described in the OAFC application. This lane emphasizes rapid MOUD assessment linkage for eligible youth ages 16+ (14-day target), evidence-based recovery support delivery (including contingency management operations and adolescent-focused treatment elements), naloxone education and distribution to youth and families, and Certified Family Partner navigation to stabilize bystanders and home environments that determine relapse and overdose risk. OAFC resources will also support required OAFC performance reporting tied to CCRB's stated targets (e.g., assessment timeliness, treatment initiation, stabilization completion, continuing-care engagement, and naloxone distribution outputs), while maintaining distinct cost objectives and documentation controls to prevent duplication with any future philanthropic or federal awards.

### Hogg Capacity-Specific Skin

> **[NEEDED]**: Exact Hogg RFP name must be inserted before submission.

Hogg funding will be used exclusively for non-clinical organizational capacity deliverables that strengthen CCRB's mental health and family-support operating system without paying for direct clinical services or duplicating OAFC-funded service delivery. This includes building and documenting CCRB's supervision and training infrastructure (peer supervision protocols, onboarding curriculum, role clarity for RSPS/MHPS/Certified Family Partners, and trauma-informed practice standards), strengthening data and quality assurance systems (measurement framework, dashboards, documentation standards, and continuous improvement routines), and formalizing cross-system partnership workflows that improve continuity of care from court to community. All Hogg-funded costs will be coded to distinct capacity deliverables with auditable outputs (manuals, training logs, QA tools, data system artifacts) to ensure the grant supplements rather than replaces OAFC services and remains compliant with the specific restrictions of the selected Hogg initiative.

### OJJDP-Specific Skin

OJJDP funding will be used to expand CCRB's juvenile justice impact and federal-grade accountability by supporting CCRB components that align to the selected OJJDP solicitation's diversion and community-integration outcomes, while financing the additional evaluation and reporting load required by DOJ/OJP systems. This lane will fund the juvenile justice expansion modules [NEEDED: name modules required by solicitation], strengthen fidelity monitoring and performance measurement processes, and ensure CCRB's data/reporting workflows meet JustGrants requirements (progress/performance reporting and financial reporting). OJJDP dollars will be tracked under separate cost objectives and coded timekeeping rules consistent with 2 CFR 200 documentation standards, with explicit exclusions preventing OAFC-funded peer contact hours, naloxone procurement/distribution, and participant support costs from being charged to the federal award unless a documented allocation method is approved and supported by records.

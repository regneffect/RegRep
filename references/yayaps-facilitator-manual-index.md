## Training Objectives

1. Increase knowledge, skills, and attitudes for supporting youth and young adults navigating mental health challenges
2. Emphasize shared/lived experience as a tool for support and connection
3. Introduce key relational tools: active listening, validation, empathy, effective communication
4. Build awareness of self-care practices and when/how to seek additional help
5. Foster environments of trust, respect, and mutual support among peers

## Complete Module Map

### DAY 1

#### Module 1: Welcome + Values Activity
- **Duration**: ~60 minutes
- **Pages**: 10-20
- **Content Summary**: Introductions, ground rules, group agreements, values identification activity
- **Key Activities**:
  - Icebreaker / introductions
  - Group agreements (collaborative norms setting)
  - Values card sort or identification activity
- **Facilitator Notes**: Sets tone for psychological safety; establishes peer-to-peer dynamic

#### Module 2: Who Am I?
- **Duration**: ~45 minutes
- **Pages**: 21-29
- **Content Summary**: Identity exploration, self-awareness building, personal strengths
- **Key Activities**:
  - Identity mapping / "Who Am I?" worksheet
  - Personal strengths identification
  - Small group sharing
- **Facilitator Notes**: Builds foundation for "shared lived experience" concept without labeling it clinically

#### Module 3: What is Peer Support?
- **Duration**: ~60 minutes
- **Pages**: 30-45
- **Content Summary**: Definition and principles of peer support, roles vs. professional support, boundaries introduction
- **Key Activities**:
  - Defining peer support collaboratively
  - Role comparison: peer supporter vs. counselor vs. friend
  - Scenarios: "Is this peer support?"
- **Facilitator Notes**: Core conceptual module; references Harrington framework and NAPS standards

#### Module 4: Active Listening
- **Duration**: ~60 minutes
- **Pages**: 46-60
- **Content Summary**: Active listening skills, verbal/nonverbal cues, reflective listening, barriers to listening
- **Key Activities**:
  - Listening skills demonstration
  - Paired practice exercises
  - Barrier identification brainstorm
- **Facilitator Notes**: High-practice module; pairs rotate for multiple reps

#### Module 5: Empathy
- **Duration**: ~45 minutes
- **Pages**: 61-73
- **Content Summary**: Empathy vs. sympathy, perspective-taking, empathic responding
- **Key Activities**:
  - Empathy vs. sympathy video/discussion
  - Empathic response practice
  - Scenario cards with guided practice
- **Facilitator Notes**: Builds directly on Module 4 listening skills

---

### DAY 2

#### Module 6: Communication Styles
- **Duration**: ~60 minutes
- **Pages**: 74-88
- **Content Summary**: Assertive, passive, aggressive, and passive-aggressive communication; "I" statements
- **Key Activities**:
  - Communication style self-assessment
  - "I" statement construction practice
  - Role-play scenarios by communication style
- **Facilitator Notes**: Participants identify their default style; practice shifting to assertive

#### Module 7: Effective Communication
- **Duration**: ~60 minutes
- **Pages**: 89-105
- **Content Summary**: Deepening communication skills, de-escalation basics, nonverbal communication awareness
- **Key Activities**:
  - Nonverbal communication exercise
  - De-escalation techniques introduction
  - Practice conversations with feedback
- **Facilitator Notes**: Extends Module 6; adds complexity with conflict scenarios

#### Module 8: Problem-Solving and Goal Setting
- **Duration**: ~60 minutes
- **Pages**: 106-120
- **Content Summary**: Structured problem-solving process, SMART goals, supporting peers in goal-setting
- **Key Activities**:
  - Problem-solving steps walkthrough
  - SMART goal setting worksheet
  - Peer coaching practice (helping someone else set a goal)
- **Facilitator Notes**: Practical skills module; participants leave with a personal goal

#### Module 9: Setting Boundaries
- **Duration**: ~45 minutes
- **Pages**: 121-133
- **Content Summary**: Types of boundaries, recognizing boundary violations, asserting boundaries as a peer supporter
- **Key Activities**:
  - Boundary types identification
  - "Where's the line?" scenario discussion
  - Practice saying no / setting limits
- **Facilitator Notes**: Critical for peer supporter safety and sustainability

#### Module 10: Self-Care
- **Duration**: ~45 minutes
- **Pages**: 134-146
- **Content Summary**: Self-care dimensions, burnout warning signs, creating a personal self-care plan
- **Key Activities**:
  - Self-care wheel assessment
  - Burnout warning signs checklist
  - Personal self-care plan creation
- **Facilitator Notes**: Pairs with Module 9 (boundaries); emphasizes that self-care is not optional for peer supporters

---

### DAY 3

#### Module 11: Crisis Awareness
- **Duration**: ~75 minutes
- **Pages**: 147-165
- **Content Summary**: Recognizing crisis signs, when to refer, mandated reporting basics, safety planning awareness
- **Key Activities**:
  - Crisis warning signs identification
  - Role of peer supporter in crisis (and its limits)
  - When and how to connect to professional support
  - Safety planning overview
- **Facilitator Notes**: Heaviest content module; requires careful facilitation; emphasize peer supporters are NOT crisis counselors

#### Module 12: Building Community
- **Duration**: ~60 minutes
- **Pages**: 166-178
- **Content Summary**: Community asset mapping, resource identification, sustaining peer support beyond training
- **Key Activities**:
  - Community resource mapping
  - Planning for peer support in participants' own settings
  - Group action planning
- **Facilitator Notes**: Transition from skills to application; forward-looking

#### Module 13: Closing + Reflection
- **Duration**: ~45 minutes
- **Pages**: 179-190
- **Content Summary**: Training synthesis, certificate presentation, commitment to practice, evaluation
- **Key Activities**:
  - Full-group reflection (STAR format applicable)
  - Letter to future self
  - Certificate ceremony
  - Training evaluation
- **Facilitator Notes**: Celebratory tone; gather evaluation data for continuous improvement

## Adaptation Notes for SCBF 2.0

### Shared Content Core
All 13 modules contain content adaptable to SCBF 2.0 editions. The core concepts (peer support definition, active listening, empathy, communication, boundaries, self-care, crisis awareness) are edition-agnostic.

### Edition-Specific Considerations

| Edition | Key Adaptations Needed |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Community-Based (90 min)** | Modules can largely maintain original depth; combine shorter modules (e.g., 5+9 or 2+5) into single sessions; add community partner integration points |
| **School-Based (45-50 min)** | Each module needs splitting into 2-3 class periods; TEKS alignment mapping required; remove or adapt content not appropriate for school setting; add grade-level differentiation |
| **After-School (60 min)** | Compress activities; Jacob's Law compliance review for crisis content (Module 11 especially); frame as enrichment not instruction; energizer activities between dense content |

### Citation Handling
- Harrington framework references appear in Modules 3, 4, 5, 11
- NAPS standards referenced in Modules 3, 6, 9, 12
- All citations move to facilitator-only materials per standing rules
- Participant-facing content uses concept language without naming frameworks

### STAR Prompt Integration Points
Every module's reflection/discussion section should be restructured to STAR format:
- Module closing reflections -> STAR structured
- Scenario debriefs -> STAR structured
- Goal-setting reviews -> STAR structured

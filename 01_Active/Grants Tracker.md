# Grants Tracker

Live grants pipeline for Credible Messengers United. Claude updates this during capture processing and weekly review.

**Status key:** `Open` (accepting now) · `Closing soon` · `Closed — watch next cycle` · `Forecast` (announced, not yet open) · `Partnership angle` (CMU not directly eligible; pursue via a partner/subaward) · `Verify` (details from web search — confirm on official link before relying on them).

> ⚠️ As of **2026-06-17**, several FY25 federal cycles below have just closed. They are kept here because they are **annual** programs — the point is to be ready and early for the next round. The live grant database (84k+ opportunities, surfaces what is open *today*) was rate-limited when this was built — re-run the Granted search or check **grantedai.com** for currently-open grants.

## Lane 1 — Reentry & Peer Support

### Second Chance Act — Community-Based Reentry Program (DOJ/BJA)
- **Fit:** Strong. Mentoring + transitional services for moderate-to-high-risk returning adults, pre/post release — squarely CMU's credible-messenger model.
- **Amount:** ~$900K–$1.5M (FY25 cycle).
- **Eligibility:** Nonprofits (incl. tribal), federally recognized tribes.
- **Status:** FY25 cycle **closed** (~May 2026). This is an annual program — **target the FY26 NOFO** and prepare early.
- **Next step:** Set a watch for the FY26 solicitation; start a reusable Second Chance narrative now while there's time.
- **Source:** https://bja.ojp.gov/funding/opportunities/o-bja-2025-172499

## Lane 2 — Youth / Quest Mentoring

### OJJDP National Mentoring Programs (DOJ/OJJDP) ⭐
- **Fit:** Strong for Quest mentoring with HCJPD-connected and neighborhood youth.
- **Status:** **Upcoming, not closed.** This is a **fall** cycle — FY25 posted Sept 18, 2025 and closed Oct 27 / Nov 3, 2025. Expect the FY26 NOFO **~September 2026**, deadline **~late Oct–early Nov 2026** (~3 months out).
- **Next step:** Prep now — youth-mentoring model, HCJPD partnership letters, outcomes, budget skeleton. Watch the OJJDP funding page from **August 2026**. See [[Grant Calendar]].
- **Source:** https://www.ojp.gov/funding/docs/ojjdp-2025-172464

### OJJDP Juvenile Justice System Enhancements (DOJ/OJJDP)
- **Fit:** Medium — diversion / system-improvement framing.
- **Status:** FY25 **closed** (~Jun 8–15, 2026). Watch FY26.
- **Source:** https://ojjdp.ojp.gov/funding/opportunities/o-ojjdp-2025-172544

## Lane 3 — Smart Choices, Bright Futures (Substance-Use Prevention)

### SAMHSA youth substance-use prevention (FY26 forecast)
- **Fit:** Strong — youth awareness, screening, prevention education align with the 8-module curriculum.
- **Status:** **Forecast** on SAMHSA's FY26 NOFO dashboard; cycles anticipated around April/May. **Verify open dates.**
- **Also:** SAMHSA Block Grants flow through **Texas HHSC** — a possible subaward/partnership lane.
- **Source:** https://www.samhsa.gov/grants/grants-dashboard/forecasts

## Lane 4 — Hotel / Recovery & Reentry Housing

### SAMHSA Youth Recovery Housing supplemental ($43M)
- **Fit:** Strong on mission, but funding flows to **State Opioid Response (SOR) grantees** (states), not directly to CMU.
- **Status:** **Partnership angle** — approach Texas HHSC/SOR about expanding youth (18–24) recovery housing via CMU.
- **Source:** https://www.samhsa.gov/newsroom/press-announcements/20250825/samhsa-announces-43m-supplemental-funding-support-youth-recovery-housing-services

### HUD FYI / FUP vouchers + TX DFPS Chafee / TAY
- **Fit:** Medium — rental assistance & transitional living for youth aging out of foster care (18–24).
- **Status:** **Partnership angle** — access via the local Public Housing Authority and DFPS contracts/referrals, not a direct competitive grant.
- **Sources:** https://www.huduser.gov/portal/youth_foster_care.html · https://www.dfps.texas.gov/Child_Protection/Youth_and_Young_Adults/Transitional_Living/youth_housing.asp

## Notes & Discipline

- Confirm every deadline/amount on the **official link** before acting — the rows above marked *Verify* came from web snippets.
- For grants that are direct-eligible, build a **reusable core narrative** (need, model, outcomes, budget) once, then tailor per funder.
- For *Partnership angle* items, the move is relationship-building (TX HHSC, PHA, DFPS), not a cold application.
- Re-run the live grant database (or grantedai.com) to capture what is **open right now** — this tracker leans on annual/recurring programs because most current federal cycles just closed.

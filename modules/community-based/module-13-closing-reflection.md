# Module 13: Closing + Reflection
**Edition:** Community-Based | **Duration:** 90 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

1. Synthesize key concepts, skills, and personal insights gained across all training modules.
2. Articulate specific actions they will take to apply their learning in their communities.
3. Identify ongoing support systems, community partnerships, and resources available beyond the training.
4. Reflect on their personal growth journey using a structured reflection format.
5. Commit to concrete next steps that strengthen their communities and sustain their own development.

## Facilitator Preparation Notes

> **INTERNAL -- NOT FOR PARTICIPANT DISTRIBUTION**

### Framework Alignment

This closing module aligns with the SCBF 2.0 framework's emphasis on sustained community engagement, reflective practice, and participant empowerment. The session synthesizes learning across all prior modules and positions participants as active contributors to community strengthening.

### Tone and Approach

- This is a **celebratory session**. Lead with warmth, pride, and affirmation.
- Honor every participant's journey regardless of attendance consistency or engagement level. Avoid language that ranks or compares participants.
- Be prepared for emotional responses. Closing rituals can surface powerful feelings about personal growth, community belonging, and transitions.
- Model vulnerability in your own reflections. If you share, keep it brief and redirect the spotlight to participants.

### Evaluation Strategy

- The post-training evaluation is a critical data-collection point for continuous improvement and funder reporting. Ensure every participant completes it.
- Frame evaluation as a gift to future participants, not a test of the current group.
- If using digital evaluation tools, have a backup paper version ready in case of connectivity issues.
- Gather both quantitative (scaled items) and qualitative (open-ended) data.

### Logistical Preparation

- **Arrive early** to set up the room with a celebratory atmosphere: arrange seating in a circle or horseshoe, display the journey timeline, set out refreshments, and test audio equipment.
- Pre-sign all certificates. If a program director or community partner leader will co-sign, coordinate in advance.
- Confirm community partner representatives' attendance at least 48 hours before the session.
- Pre-address envelopes for the "Letter to My Future Self" activity if you plan to mail them (typically 3-6 months post-training).
- Review participant names for correct pronunciation before the certificate ceremony.

### Harrington Framework Connection

This module operationalizes Harrington's principle of reflective closure: participants consolidate learning through structured recall, articulate transfer intentions, and are positioned within a continuing ecosystem of support. The STAR reflection structure reinforces Harrington's emphasis on experiential learning integration.

### NAPS Alignment

- **NAPS Standard 4: Youth Development and Leadership** -- Closing reflection affirms participant growth and emerging leadership identity.
- **NAPS Standard 6: Community Engagement** -- Community partner presence at closing reinforces the ecosystem of ongoing support.
- **NAPS Standard 8: Program Sustainability** -- Evaluation data gathered supports continuous improvement cycles required under national quality standards.

## Activities

### Activity 1: Welcome + Grounding Circle (10 minutes)

**Setup:** Participants seated in a circle. Journey timeline (Modules 1-13) displayed prominently. Soft music playing as participants arrive.

**Facilitator Script:**

> "Welcome to our final session together. Take a look around this circle. These are the people who have walked this journey with you -- who have shared, listened, challenged, and grown alongside you. Today is about honoring that journey, celebrating your growth, and looking ahead to what comes next."

**Grounding Activity: "One Word"**

Go around the circle and ask each participant to share one word that describes how they feel arriving at this final session. The facilitator participates as well.

> "In one word, how are you feeling as we begin our last session together?"

After the round, acknowledge the range of emotions:

> "All of those feelings are welcome here today. Whether you're feeling proud, bittersweet, excited, or something else entirely -- that's part of what makes this moment real."

**Transition:** Briefly walk participants through the session flow so they know what to expect. Emphasize that this is their time.

---

### Activity 2: Full-Group Reflection -- "Our Collective Journey" (20 minutes)

**Purpose:** Guide participants through a structured reflection that synthesizes insights from the entire training.

**Part A: Journey Mapping (8 minutes)**

Distribute two colors of sticky notes to each participant.

> "On the [first color] sticky notes, write down the things you are **taking with you** from this experience -- skills, insights, relationships, mindset shifts. One idea per sticky note. Write as many as you'd like."
>
> "On the [second color] sticky notes, write down the things you are **leaving behind** -- old habits, limiting beliefs, fears, or assumptions that no longer serve you. One idea per sticky note."

Give participants 5 minutes to write. Then invite them to place their sticky notes on the large poster paper on the wall, grouped by color.

**Part B: Gallery Reflection (4 minutes)**

Invite participants to stand and read what others have written. Encourage quiet reflection. No discussion yet.

**Part C: Group Discussion Using STAR Format (8 minutes)**

Bring participants back to the circle. Use the following guided prompts to facilitate a full-group conversation. Not everyone needs to answer every prompt -- invite willing voices.

> **Situation:** "Think back to your very first session. What was going on in your life at that time? What brought you here?"
>
> **Task:** "What did you hope to get out of this training? What were you working toward?"
>
> **Action:** "What is something specific you did during this training that pushed you outside your comfort zone or helped you grow?"
>
> **Result:** "How are you different now compared to when you started? What has changed in how you see yourself, your community, or your role in it?"

**Facilitator Note:** Validate every response. Use bridging language like "Thank you for sharing that" and "That connects to what [name] said about..." to weave a collective narrative.

---

### Activity 3: Letter to My Future Self (15 minutes)

**Purpose:** Participants write a personal letter capturing their current commitments, hopes, and state of mind. The letter will be mailed to them 3-6 months later as a reminder of their intentions.

**Facilitator Script:**

> "You're going to write a letter to yourself -- the version of you who will open this letter a few months from now. This letter is private. No one else will read it. Be honest. Be kind to yourself. And be specific."

**Distribute:** Stationery and envelopes.

**Prompt Card (distribute to each participant):**

---

**Letter to My Future Self**

*Date: _______________*

*Dear Future Me,*

*Use the prompts below to guide your letter, or write freely.*

1. **Right now, I feel...** *(Describe your current emotional state as you complete this training.)*

2. **The most important thing I learned is...** *(Name the insight, skill, or realization that stands out most.)*

3. **I am committing to...** *(Be specific. What will you do differently in your daily life, your community, or your relationships?)*

4. **When things get hard, I want to remember...** *(Write a message of encouragement from your present self to your future self.)*

5. **The people I want to thank are...** *(Name them, and say why.)*

6. **Six months from now, I hope I am...** *(Describe the version of yourself you are working toward.)*

*With belief in you,*
*Me*

---

**After 12 minutes of writing time:**

> "When you're ready, seal your letter in the envelope and write your mailing address on the front. We will mail these to you in [3/6] months as a gift from your present self."

Collect sealed envelopes. Store securely.

**Purpose:** Formally recognize each participant's completion of the training.

### Activity 7: Closing Circle + Send-Off (10 minutes)

**Purpose:** Create a meaningful final moment of connection and send participants off with energy and intention.

**Closing Round -- "My Commitment in One Sentence"**

> "We're going to go around the circle one final time. In one sentence, share one commitment you're making as you leave this space. What will you carry forward?"

Allow each participant to share. The facilitator goes last.

**Group Affirmation:**

> "I'd like to ask everyone to stand. Look around this circle one more time. These are the people who believed in this work, who showed up, who did the hard and beautiful thing of growing in community. You did this. And you carry each other's stories with you."

**Optional Closing Ritual (choose one based on group culture):**

- **Linked hands moment:** Participants hold hands in silence for 10 seconds, then release with a collective exhale or word of affirmation.
- **Call and response:** Facilitator says "We came as individuals," group responds "We leave as community."
- **Song or spoken word:** A participant or community partner leads a brief song, poem, or spoken-word piece.

**Group Photo**

> "Before we head to the refreshments -- let's get a group photo! Everyone gather in."

Take the photo. Share digitally with participants afterward (with appropriate permissions).

**Celebration and Refreshments**

Release participants to refreshments, informal conversation, and continued connection. Community partner representatives remain available for one-on-one conversations.

## STAR Reflection Prompts

This module's reflection prompts are designed to help participants synthesize their **entire training journey** across all 13 modules. Use these in the full-group reflection activity (Activity 2) and encourage participants to draw on them during the Letter to My Future Self (Activity 3).

### Prompt Set 1: The Full Arc

- **Situation:** "When you first walked into Module 1, what was happening in your life? What circumstances brought you to this training?"
- **Task:** "What did you hope to accomplish, learn, or change by participating? What goal -- spoken or unspoken -- were you carrying?"
- **Action:** "Name one specific thing you did during this training that you are proud of. It could be something you said, a risk you took, or a moment when you showed up differently than you would have before."
- **Result:** "What is different now? In yourself, in your relationships, in how you see your role in your community?"

### Prompt Set 2: Skills in Action

- **Situation:** "Think about a real situation in your life -- at home, at work, in your neighborhood -- where you recently faced a challenge."
- **Task:** "What did the situation require of you? What needed to happen?"
- **Action:** "Did you use any of the skills or strategies you learned in this training to handle it? What did you do?"
- **Result:** "How did it turn out? What would you do the same or differently next time?"

### Prompt Set 3: Community Impact

- **Situation:** "Consider your community right now -- the people, places, and systems around you."
- **Task:** "What does your community need from people who have been through an experience like this?"
- **Action:** "What is one step you can take in the next 30 days to contribute to your community based on what you've learned?"
- **Result:** "If you take that step, what do you hope the impact will be -- for you and for others?"

### Prompt Set 4: Relationships and Connection

- **Situation:** "Think about the relationships you've formed or deepened during this training."
- **Task:** "What was it about this environment that made it possible to connect with others?"
- **Action:** "What did you do to build trust, show support, or be a good participant in this group?"
- **Result:** "How will you sustain those connections after today? What will you carry from these relationships?"

## Assessment / Check for Understanding

### Post-Training Assessment Strategy

Module 13 serves as the final assessment point. Assessment in this session is **holistic and reflective** rather than quiz-based. Data is gathered through three channels:

**1. Observation-Based Facilitator Assessment**

During the full-group reflection and closing circle, the facilitator assesses the following indicators:

| Indicator | Evidence to Look For |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Synthesis of learning** | Participant references concepts or skills from multiple modules, not just the most recent sessions |
| **Self-awareness and growth** | Participant articulates specific ways they have changed, with concrete examples |
| **Community orientation** | Participant connects their learning to community impact, not just personal benefit |
| **Commitment to action** | Participant names specific, realistic next steps rather than vague aspirations |
| **Relational connection** | Participant acknowledges the contributions of peers and the value of the group experience |

Complete the facilitator observation form after the session, not during (to remain present with the group).

**2. Participant Self-Assessment (Embedded in Evaluation)**

The pre/post confidence ratings in the evaluation form (Section 2) provide quantitative self-assessment data. Compare with any pre-training baseline data collected during Module 1.

**3. Letter to My Future Self (Delayed Assessment)**

The sealed letter serves as a delayed self-assessment tool. When mailed back to participants in 3-6 months, it creates an opportunity for self-comparison between intentions and actions. Consider pairing the mailing with a brief follow-up survey.

## Community Partner Integration

### Purpose

The closing session is a high-visibility opportunity for community partners to reinforce the message that the participant's journey does not end with the training. Partners present ongoing pathways, resources, and support structures that sustain growth beyond the program.

### Pre-Session Coordination

- Invite community partner representatives at least **two weeks** in advance. Provide a brief overview of the session flow and their role.
- Ask each partner to prepare:
  - A 2-minute overview of their organization and how participants can stay connected.
  - Printed resource cards, flyers, or information packets for each participant.
  - At least one specific, upcoming opportunity participants can engage with immediately (e.g., a volunteer day, a workshop, a mentoring program, a job fair).
- Confirm partner attendance **48 hours** before the session.

### During the Session (Activity 4: Community Partner Moment -- 10 minutes)

**Facilitator Introduction:**

> "One of the things that makes this program powerful is that you're not doing this alone. You're connected to a network of people and organizations that believe in you and want to see you succeed. I'd like to invite our community partners to share a few words about how you can stay connected after today."

**Partner Presentations (2-3 minutes each, maximum of 3 partners):**

Each partner briefly shares:
1. Who they are and what they do.
2. One specific way participants can engage with them in the next 30 days.
3. How to contact them (distribute resource cards).

**Facilitator Bridge:**

> "Thank you to our partners for being here today and for being part of your journey. Their information is in the resource packet you'll receive. I encourage you to reach out -- these connections are a real extension of the work you've done here."

### Resource Packet Contents (Prepared in Advance)

Each participant receives a packet or folder containing:

- [ ] Community partner contact information cards
- [ ] List of upcoming community events and opportunities
- [ ] Local resource directory (mental health services, employment support, educational opportunities, civic engagement)
- [ ] Facilitator and/or program coordinator contact information for continued support
- [ ] Alumni network information (if applicable) -- online group, quarterly meetups, mentoring opportunities
- [ ] Any relevant referral forms or sign-up sheets for partner programs

### Post-Session Partner Follow-Up

Within one week of the closing session:

- Send a thank-you message to each attending community partner.
- Share aggregate (non-identifying) feedback from evaluations related to community partnership.
- Discuss potential referrals or warm handoffs for participants who expressed interest.
- Begin planning for alumni engagement touchpoints (30-day check-in, 90-day reunion, letter mailing).

## Core Frameworks

### Bottom-Up Justice

Bottom-Up Justice means people closest to the harm should help design the repair.

It focuses on:
- peer-led systems
- reentry infrastructure
- community power
- no-entry and no-reentry pathways
- organizational models that reduce dependence on punishment systems
- practical alternatives to surveillance-based success metrics

### No-Entry Infrastructure Model

The goal is to prevent durable criminal-legal contact and, when contact happens, keep it from becoming permanent punishment.

Layers:
1. Survival Floor — housing, food, utilities, ID, transportation, emergency cash
2. Crisis & Conflict Interruption — non-police crisis response, violence interruption, family conflict support
3. Navigation Power — court navigation, participatory defense, peer navigation, benefits support, school/CPS advocacy
4. Healing & Stabilization — trauma care, substance-use continuity, mental health support, peer support
5. Economic Belonging — jobs, training, credentials, employer networks, record navigation
6. Civic Standing — rights restoration, expunction, leadership pipelines, community governance

Outcome:
Full membership without surveillance.
